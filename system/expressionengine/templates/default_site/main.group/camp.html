FAQs


What is Camp WOW? And where is it?

Since 2001, Camp WOW has been a place for people to encounter God in a meaningful and lasting way. Over thousands of students have attended since it’s establishment. Camp WOW, located near Gerty, Oklahoma, has partnered with Journey Church for a number of years and has always provided a safe and fun environment for our students to experience Jesus. For more information visit campwow.com



What's the goal of camp?

Although the themes change yearly, the goal is all the same, that we would see students take a step toward owning their faith and live into their God given potential.

What is the adult/kid ratio?

1:10 (one adult for every 10 students)



What are the sleeping arrangements?

Dorm/Bunk Bed style. Each Dorm is comprised of 4 bays, and each bay can sleep 58 students (There are 58 beds). Students are separated by grade and gender. Depending on the amount of students registered per grade, students may be paired with the students in the next grade. For example: 5th and 6th grade boys in Bay 1 instead of just 5th grade boys in Bay 1. This is done to maximize the lodging space available while keeping your students in mind. You will not see 5th grade students with 12th grade students. Small Group Adult Leaders will also be lodging with your students.

All adults have had background checks, have served in our ministry is some sort of capacity, and have gone through our leader training classes.



What is the food like?

The meals are well balanced and delicious. In the cafe, alongside the meal, there is always a salad bar, and a potato bar. If for some reason, your child doesn’t like the food served, the camp has a “snack shack” that serves pizza, chicken strips, fries, and nachos. But in our experience, kids head to the snack bar because they want ice cream.

Are students allowed electronics?

ONLY phones, but it is regulated. Students are allowed to utilize their phones on the bus, during free time, and before bed to call home (if needed). Other than that, they turn in their phones to their small group leaders. This isn’t because we want to punish your child. It is because we want students to engage with their friends and the environment around them.



How can I contact my child if I need to?

We ask that you contact your child’s pastor. They will always have their phone on them. There is spotty reception at the water front and the worship center, so if you are unable to get ahold of them, the camp has a land line that you can call and they will take a message and run it to them. Once receiving the message, the pastor will immediately call you.



What happens if my child gets injured?

Should that happen, the camp has an on location nurse. All injuries are seen by the nurse. Anything bigger than a small scrape, you will be notified.



How far away is the nearest hospital?

The nearest hospital is located in Ada, OK.
